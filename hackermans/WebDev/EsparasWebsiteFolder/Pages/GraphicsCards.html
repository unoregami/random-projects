Graphics Cards

Graphics Card, also known as Graphics Processing Unit(GPU) or Video Card, is an output device that sends appropriate information to the computer's screen for display. Some GPU offers port availability of HDMI, DisplayPort, DVI, or VGA Connectors. With a Graphics Card installed into your system, it gives a big boost in performance since it takes the burden of all the video processing from the CPU. The card's memory amount varies depending on the manufacturer's design. Video Cards use Graphics Double Data Rate (GDDR) to optimize graphics performance, it is built to handle a higher bandwidth compared to plain DDR RAM.
